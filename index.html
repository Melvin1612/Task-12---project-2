K. Satchidanandan

~~~ A Tamil Poet ~~~

Biography

K. Satchidanandan was born on May 28, 1946, in Kerala, India. He completed his education in Kerala and later obtained a Ph.D. in post-structuralist literary theory from Mahatma Gandhi University. Satchidanandan has had a diverse career as a poet, critic, translator, and editor. He has worked in various capacities, including serving as the editor of Indian Literature, the flagship journal of the Sahitya Akademi (India's National Academy of Letters).

Works

Satchidanandan has published several poetry collections in both Tamil and English. His poetry explores a wide range of themes, including love, spirituality, politics, nature, and the human condition. Notable poetry collections include "Anchored Earth," "While I Write," "Misplaced Objects," and "Second Nature". Satchidanandan has also authored essays, literary criticism, and travelogues. His prose writings delve into various topics such as literature, culture, society, and contemporary issues. His book "Crossing the Frontiers: Critical Essays on Indian Writing in English" is widely acclaimed. Satchidanandan is highly regarded for his translations of Tamil poetry into English and Malayalam. He has translated the works of renowned Tamil poets like Bharathiyar, Thiruvalluvar, and Subramania Bharati. These translations have played a significant role in making Tamil literature accessible to wider audiences.
"Poetry is the language of the soul, the voice that emerges from the depths of our being to express the inexpressible, to reveal the hidden truths, and to connect us to the vastness of the universe."-K. Satchidanandan

The Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award is a prestigious literary award presented by the Kerala Sahitya Akademi, the state academy for Malayalam literature in Kerala, India

The Gangadhar Meher National Award is bestowed by the Government of Odisha, India, in recognition of outstanding contributions to Indian literature.

2009

The Kuvempu National Award is presented by the Kuvempu University, Karnataka, in honor of outstanding contributions to literature and culture.
